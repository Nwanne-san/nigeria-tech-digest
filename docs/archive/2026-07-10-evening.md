### Abiodun Raises Funding, Judicial Reform Concerns as State Police Bill Advances
**Source:** Guardian Nigeria

**What happened**
Ogun State Governor Dapo Abiodun has called for extensive judicial, fiscal, and institutional reforms to ensure the success of the proposed State Police system. Speaking at a THISDAY/ARISE TV Town Hall Meeting on the State Police Bill, Governor Abiodun emphasized that the initiative must go beyond merely creating new security structures. He stressed the need to address the legal and financial frameworks required to sustain such a system, arguing that an efficient state police would lead to more arrests and prosecutions, necessitating a stronger judiciary for speedy trials. He also highlighted funding disparities among states, noting that Ogun State, despite being an industrial hub, receives low allocations from the Federation Account, which could undermine the effectiveness of state police if not addressed through an equitable funding framework.

**Deep Lore / Context**
This discussion on State Police is a significant development in the long-standing debate over Nigeria's centralized policing system, which has been widely criticized for its inefficiency and inability to effectively address localized security challenges. The call for State Police reflects a growing consensus among state governors and various stakeholders that the current structure is inadequate, especially in the face of escalating insecurity. President Bola Ahmed Tinubu's support for the devolution of policing powers, as noted by Governor Abiodun, marks a potential shift towards true federalism, a principle that has been advocated for decades. This move directly connects to the ongoing storyline of **Nigeria's Infrastructure for Insecurity Response**, as it represents a fundamental structural reform aimed at improving security capabilities at the sub-national level.

However, Governor Abiodun's concerns about judicial reform and funding are critical and highlight the complex challenges inherent in such a monumental shift. The current judicial system is already burdened, and an increase in arrests would exacerbate this without concurrent reforms, potentially leading to prolonged detentions and further erosion of public trust. The funding issue is equally contentious; states with lower internally generated revenue (IGR) fear they would be unable to adequately equip and pay their police forces, creating a two-tiered security system where wealthier states are better protected. This raises questions about equity and national cohesion, and whether the federal government will provide significant support or if it will be left entirely to states. The establishment of independent Police Complaints Commissions, as advocated by Abiodun, is also crucial for preventing abuse of power, a historical concern with policing in Nigeria.

### Frontier and Center: Who evaluates the evaluations?
**Source:** Google Cloud Blog

**What happened**
Google Data Cloud's frontier AI team has published an insightful blog post on the critical challenge of systematically evaluating AI agent performance, particularly in data discovery and retrieval. They introduce an approach rooted in information theory, leveraging "surprisal" to modulate the difficulty of evaluation cases. This meta-benchmark, called Discovery Bench, generates "easy" and "hard" variations of queries, allowing researchers to map an agent's capabilities and identify "cliffs" where performance drops off sharply. For example, removing a distinguishing token like "TLE" from a query about satellite data can cause an agent to fail entirely, even if it performs perfectly on the neutral phrasing. This method provides a more nuanced understanding of agent performance than traditional pass/fail benchmarks.

**Deep Lore / Context**
This deep dive into AI agent evaluation is highly relevant to the **AI Governance and Regulation Gap** and **AI Agents in Enterprise Observability** storylines. As AI agents become increasingly autonomous and are deployed for critical tasks like root cause analysis (predicted to reach 98% adoption in enterprises within two years), understanding their limitations and ensuring their reliability is paramount. The current methods of evaluating AI agents are often insufficient, providing only a superficial "pass/fail" score that doesn't reveal *why* an agent succeeds or fails, or under what conditions its performance degrades.

The concept of "surprisal" and the Discovery Bench framework directly address this gap by providing a rigorous, quantitative method to assess an agent's robustness to ambiguity and vagueness. This is crucial for enterprise applications where real-world queries are often imperfectly phrased. The discovery of "cliffs" in agent performance, where a small change in query ambiguity leads to complete failure, highlights significant security and reliability risks. This also connects to the broader discussion around **AI Security Vulnerabilities**, as an agent failing to retrieve correct data due to a subtly ambiguous query could lead to incorrect decisions or system failures. The industry's convergence on meta-benchmarking, as noted in the article, signifies a growing recognition of the need for more sophisticated evaluation techniques to build trustworthy and resilient AI systems.
