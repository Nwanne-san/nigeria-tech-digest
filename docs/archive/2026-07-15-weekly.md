## Nigeria: The Big Picture

### Fiscal Accountability Under Intense Scrutiny

The past few days have seen a dramatic escalation in the scrutiny of Nigeria's public finances, revealing deep-seated issues of transparency and accountability. The "Nigeria's Off-Budget Spending Controversy," initially centered on allegations surrounding the purported Presidential Fiscal and Investment Policy Council (PFIPC) and N8.83 trillion in off-budget spending, has broadened significantly. On July 13 (morning), police moved to arraign Adeniyi Adeyemi, the alleged head of the PFIPC, a concrete step towards addressing the scam. However, the same day (evening), a Senate motion to investigate the agency's inclusion in the 2026 budget was controversially defeated, with the Senate spokesperson claiming lawmakers aren't responsible for background checks. This legislative reluctance was quickly followed by a staggering revelation from the Customs Service: N34 trillion in duty waivers granted in 2025, significantly impacting national revenue (July 13 evening). The controversy intensified on July 14 (morning) when Adeyemi publicly denied hiding and sensationally claimed he borrowed N400 million to secure his appointment to the questionable PFIPC, calling for an independent panel. The most significant development came on July 14 (evening) and continued on July 15 (morning), with the House of Representatives Public Accounts Committee (PAC) launching an aggressive probe. They directed the Accountant-General of the Federation (OAGF) to disclose N5.3 trillion in alleged unremitted operating surplus from the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) and Nigerian National Petroleum Company Limited (NNPCL), alongside unauthorized deductions from agencies like UBEC and NASENI. The OAGF defended these as "temporary borrowing arrangements," a justification rejected by the committee. This pattern reveals a systemic crisis in public finance management, characterized by opaque practices, significant revenue leakages, and a growing assertiveness from the legislative arm to enforce fiscal discipline. The trajectory is one of rapidly escalating pressure for transparency, with the potential for high-profile revelations and institutional reforms.
