### Securing the AI Supply Chain on GKE: Introducing k8s-aibom for Automated AI BOMs
**Source:** Google Cloud Blog

**What happened:**
Google Cloud has open-sourced `k8s-aibom`, a lightweight, unprivileged Kubernetes controller designed to secure the AI supply chain by automatically detecting running AI runtimes and generating standard CycloneDX Machine Learning Bill of Materials (ML-BOMs). This tool addresses the challenge of "shadow AI"—workloads deployed by developers without formal registration that often evade traditional security scanners. `k8s-aibom` continuously monitors the cluster API and container environments to identify AI stacks like vLLM, Triton, LangChain, AutoGen, and vector databases. It deploys as a single, unprivileged Deployment, requiring no developer friction, sidecars, or modifications to existing pod specifications. The tool categorizes discovered assets into "Declared," "Inferred," and "Unresolved" confidence tiers, providing audit-grade visibility and helping organizations comply with global regulatory frameworks such as the EU AI Act, NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF), and ISO/IEC 42001.

**Deep Lore / Context:**
The introduction of `k8s-aibom` is a crucial development in the "AI Security Vulnerabilities" and "AI Governance and Regulation Gap" storylines, offering a practical solution to a growing problem in enterprise AI adoption. As AI workloads proliferate across cloud environments, the challenge of maintaining visibility, ensuring compliance, and mitigating security risks has become paramount. "Shadow AI" poses a significant threat, as unauthorized or unmonitored AI deployments can introduce vulnerabilities, intellectual property risks, and regulatory non-compliance. Traditional security tools often struggle to keep pace with the dynamic nature of AI development and deployment, leading to a deadlock between security mandates and developer agility. `k8s-aibom` breaks this deadlock by providing continuous, automated, and friction-free visibility into what AI components are actually running in a Kubernetes cluster, regardless of how they were deployed.

The tool's ability to generate standards-conformant CycloneDX 1.6 ML-BOMs is particularly significant. This standardization allows for seamless integration with broader security ecosystems and facilitates compliance with emerging global AI regulations. The "Confidence Model" (Declared, Inferred, Unresolved) is an innovative approach to distinguishing between explicit engineering intent and dynamic machine inference, providing auditors and SecOps teams with actionable intelligence. Furthermore, its design for immutability and least privilege, ensuring that BOM records are cryptographically immutable once written to external storage, addresses deep skepticism from auditors regarding the tamper-proof nature of telemetry data. This level of audit-grade evidence is essential for organizations navigating the complex landscape of AI governance, helping them safely move AI projects from pilot to production and proactively address the energy and operational safety concerns that have been part of the "AI Stock Market Exuberance and Caution" storyline.
