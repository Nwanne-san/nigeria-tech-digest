### Edo Assembly Directs LG Chairmen To Submit Security Network Payment Schedules, Passes IGR Harmonisation Bill
**Source:** Channels TV

**What happened:**
The Edo State House of Assembly today issued a directive to the chairmen of its 18 local government councils, demanding the submission of payment schedules for officers of the Edo State Security Network by July 24, 2026. This move is aimed at resolving an ongoing dispute concerning the payment of stipends to these security personnel. Speaker Rt. Hon. Blessing Agbebaku clarified that the Assembly's intervention is not a "witch-hunt" but an effort to verify payments and ensure transparency. In a related legislative action, the Assembly also passed the Edo State Revenue Harmonisation Bill into law. This new legislation establishes a unified Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) account for the state, centralizing the collection, management, control, and accounting of all state revenues and public funds. It explicitly repeals existing laws that allowed ministries, departments, agencies, and parastatals to maintain separate revenue accounts, aiming to promote transparency, accountability, and efficient cash management.

**Deep Lore / Context:**
This development marks a significant push for fiscal transparency and accountability at the state level, directly echoing the broader national conversation around financial oversight. The directive regarding the Edo State Security Network's payment schedules ties into the ongoing `State Police Debate and Implementation Challenges` storyline. As states increasingly establish their own security outfits, questions of funding, operational transparency, and accountability become paramount. The dispute over stipends highlights the practical difficulties and potential for mismanagement in these nascent local security structures. Ensuring that these local security networks are properly funded and their personnel paid transparently is crucial for their effectiveness and public trust, especially as the nation grapples with widespread insecurity.

The passage of the IGR Harmonisation Bill is a direct response to long-standing issues of fragmented revenue collection and potential leakages in public funds. This initiative connects to the overarching `Nigeria's Off-Budget Spending Controversy` storyline, which has seen intense scrutiny on fiscal transparency, particularly concerning large expenditures like the N7.13 trillion energy security spending. By centralizing IGR, Edo State aims to plug revenue gaps, improve financial planning, and reduce opportunities for corruption. This move, if effectively implemented, could serve as a model for other states struggling with similar issues, reinforcing the need for systemic reforms in budgetary oversight and public finance management across all tiers of government. The success of such a bill hinges on robust enforcement and the political will to overcome resistance from agencies accustomed to managing their own separate accounts.
