### President Tinubu Orders Probe into Alleged Fake Agency in His Office
**Source:** BBC Africa

**What happened:** President Bola Tinubu has ordered a comprehensive corruption investigation into allegations that a fictitious government agency, the Presidential Foreign Intervention Promotion Council (PFIPC), was established within his office and secured public funding. The presidency claims the letter from the president's chief of staff, Femi Gbajabiamila, creating the PFIPC was forged. A manhunt is underway for Adeniyi Adeyemi Matthew, who presented himself as the "PFIPC director general," on suspicion of forgery, impersonation, and related offenses. Adeyemi, now in hiding, maintains his innocence and alleges senior government officials demanded bribes and attempted to seize control of the council's funds, claims the presidency denies. The PFIPC, purportedly set up in 2024 to attract foreign investment, had secured office space in the Federal Secretariat, opened bank accounts with the Central Bank of Nigeria, and was allocated ₦1.3 billion ($950,000) in the 2026 Appropriation Act. However, the Accountant-General's Office states the PFIPC never had an operational account with the CBN and received no public funds or salaries.

**Deep Lore / Context:** This incident marks a significant escalation in the ongoing "Nigeria's Off-Budget Spending Controversy" storyline, which has intensified scrutiny on government transparency and governance. Allegations of N8.83 trillion in off-budget spending have already fueled public concern, and this specific case of a seemingly phantom agency with a budget allocation directly within the presidency's purview adds a concrete, alarming dimension to those broader claims. The fact that the PFIPC managed to appear in the national budget and secure physical office space within a federal complex, despite allegedly being a fabrication, points to deep systemic weaknesses in government oversight and financial controls. The investigation ordered by President Tinubu, to be conducted by the Independent Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Commission (ICPC) with a 30-day deadline, is a direct response to mounting public pressure from civil society organizations, opposition politicians, and legal experts who have demanded an independent inquiry. The president's statement emphasizes the need to protect the integrity of federal institutions against impersonation, forgery, and the exploitation of public service weaknesses.

This scandal also provides further ammunition for "Peter Obi's Political Pressure on Tinubu," as the Labour Party presidential candidate has consistently demanded President Tinubu's resignation over governance failures, often citing issues like alleged off-budget spending. The PFIPC case serves as a tangible example that validates some of the concerns raised by the opposition, putting the government on the defensive regarding its commitment to anti-corruption and fiscal discipline. The allegations by Adeniyi Adeyemi Matthew of bribery demands from senior government officials, even if denied by the presidency, further complicate the narrative and suggest a deeper web of complicity or negligence that the ICPC investigation will need to untangle. The outcome of this probe will be a critical test of the administration's resolve to tackle corruption at the highest levels and could significantly impact public trust and the political landscape as the 2027 elections draw closer.

The decision to port the compiler to Go, a language known for its performance and concurrency features, represents a strategic investment in the future scalability and developer experience of TypeScript.
